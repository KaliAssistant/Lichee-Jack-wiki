# 3D Print Cases

This guide explains how to **3D print, prepare, and assemble** the official Lichee-Jack enclosure. The case is designed for durability, portability, and daily carry, while remaining fully open-source and easy to reproduce.

## Step 1 — Print the Case

![](/assets/CASE_WITHSW.jpeg)

**Supported materials:**

* PLA+
* PETG (**strongly recommended**)
* ABS

### Recommended Print Settings

> **PETG! PETG! PETG!**

* Layer height: **0.16 – 0.20 mm**
* Infill: **30 – 50 %**
* Supports: **Only where needed**
* Orientation: **Flat face down**

> [!WARNING]
>
> Avoid **resin / SLA printing**. These materials are brittle and not suitable for heat-set inserts or daily mechanical stress.

---

## Step 2 — Install Heat-Set Inserts

1. Heat your soldering iron to **180 – 200 °C**.
2. Align the heat-set insert with the hole.
3. Press gently while heating until the insert sits flush.
4. Allow the plastic to cool completely before installing screws.

## Step 3 — Add Optical Fiber for LED Light Pipe

* Insert **plastic optic fiber** into the LED holes.
* This diffuses the internal LED into a clean external glow.
* Trim the fiber ends flush with the case surface.


![](/assets/CASE_6.jpeg)
<br/>
![](/assets/CASE_7.jpeg)


---

## Step 4 — Final Assembly

1. Place the PCB assembly into the bottom shell.
2. Insert the **switch header** into the side slot **before closing** the case.
3. Align the top shell.
4. Secure with **M2 screws**.
5. Tighten gently — **do not overtighten**.

## Design Notes

* The enclosure is optimized for **daily carry** and **field use**
* Heat-set inserts allow repeated opening without plastic wear
* Optical fiber provides visibility without external LEDs
